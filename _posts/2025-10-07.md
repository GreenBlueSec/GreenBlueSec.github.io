October is **Cybersecurity Awareness Month** and this year’s theme: **"Secure Our World."**. So... what in the heck is that all about?

Pause. We're not here to discuss what cybersecurity is all about. That is a long and particularily dry conversation. Instead, we are here to identify action items which will make you more secure (see Table 1 Below). Our goal is to improve our digital self defense. To do so, we will make a series of specific behavioral changes to improve our digital health and habits. Focus on just your accounts to start even if you have dependents or other family members who rely on you. Keep the scope focused on you go through, the list entirely, and then apply what you learned again to those you need to protect.

### ✅ Cybersecurity Defensive Improvements

| Step | Title                                         | Requirements / Notes                                                                                                                                                     |
|------|-----------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 1    | Create a Free Cloud Storage Account           | Sign up for a free service (e.g., **Google Drive**, **Proton Drive**, **Dropbox**) where you can safely store backups of important files, credentials, and documents.     |
| 2    | Create an Asset Inventory                     | - List all email accounts you actively use. <br> - Identify **critical accounts** tied to health, finance, cloud storage, work, etc. <br> - Track any 2FA/MFA methods.   |
| 3    | Delete Unused or Unnecessary Accounts         | - Close any accounts you no longer use. <br> - Back up anything important before deletion. <br> - ✨ *Bonus:* Request **data deletion** (GDPR/"Right to Be Forgotten").   |
| 4    | Review & Harden Critical Account Security     | - Enable **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** everywhere. <br> - Use **strong, unique passwords** via a password manager. <br> - Check for unauthorized logins.          |
| 5    | Set Up a Password Manager                     | Choose one: **Bitwarden**, **1Password**, **Proton Pass**, etc. <br> Import or enter all existing credentials. <br> Enable biometric unlock on mobile.                    |
| 6    | Update Devices and Software                   | Ensure your operating system, apps, browsers, and firmware are up to date. <br> Enable **auto-updates** where possible.                                                   |
| 7    | Audit Browser Extensions & Mobile Apps        | Remove unused or shady extensions and apps. <br> Check permissions — especially for access to email, files, or contacts.                                                  |
| 8    | Back Up Critical Data                         | Store backups of family photos, tax documents, passwords, or medical records. <br> Use both **cloud storage** and a **local encrypted drive** if possible.               |
| 9    | Set Up Device Encryption                      | Enable full disk encryption: **BitLocker** (Windows), **FileVault** (Mac), or device-level encryption (iOS/Android).                                                     |
| 10   | Establish a Personal Incident Response Plan   | - Know how to lock down accounts. <br> - Keep an offline copy of key recovery codes. <br> - Practice recovering from a lost/stolen device scenario.                      |
